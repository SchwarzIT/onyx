## Principles

Images are powerful tools for conveying messages and information quickly. They can communicate concepts, emotions, and information more effectively than text alone. We recommend following the below key principles when using images:

<steps>

::step
#headline
Color gamut

#default
Select images where a single primary hue (e.g., blue, green, or red) dominates the composition.

<layout-container>
<image-do-dont-card src="/images/color-gamut-pro.webp" alt="Green container" color="success">
Choose images with a harmonious, monochromatic, or analogous color profile.
</image-do-dont-card>

<image-do-dont-card src="/images/color-gamut-con.webp" alt="Multiple containers with different mixed colors" color="danger">
Don't use images with a high-saturation mix of multiple competing primary colors.
</image-do-dont-card>
</layout-container>

**Tip:** Use varying color gamuts across different pages to provide users with subtle visual cues for navigation and orientation.
::

::step
#headline
Natural lighting

#default
Prioritize soft, authentic light sources to maintain a grounded and realistic aesthetic.

<layout-container>
<image-do-dont-card src="/images/natural-light-pro.webp" alt="Two men sitting at a table looking at each other with natural sun light" color="success">
Ensure the light is naturally balanced and the subjects appear evenly lit.
</image-do-dont-card>

<image-do-dont-card src="/images/natural-light-con.webp" alt="Man sitting in a dark room in front of a computer" color="danger">
Don't use images with harsh highlights, deep shadows, or high-contrast studio lighting, especially on faces.
</image-do-dont-card>
</layout-container>

**Tip:** Avoid over-processing or applying highly stylized filters that make the image look artificial.
::

::step
#headline
Authentic snapshots

#default
Choose "candid" imagery that feels like a captured moment in time rather than a staged production.

<layout-container>
<image-do-dont-card src="/images/snapshot-pro.webp" alt="Group of people in a creative meeting room looking at a whiteboard with sticky notes" color="success">
Look for natural movements and genuine human interactions.
</image-do-dont-card>

<image-do-dont-card src="/images/snapshot-con.webp" alt="Group of people sitting at a desk looking at paper sheets with unnatural conversation" color="danger">
Don't use images with forced expressions, "stock-photo" smiles, or clearly rehearsed poses.
</image-do-dont-card>
</layout-container>

**Tip:** Every photo should feel like a real-life snapshot of a normal, unscripted action.
::

::step
#headline
Visual simplicity

#default
Focus on minimalist compositions that emphasize a single, clear message.

<layout-container>
<image-do-dont-card src="/images/visual-simplicity-pro.webp" alt="Hand holding a credit card" color="success">
Look for images with generous "negative space" or clean backgrounds.
</image-do-dont-card>

<image-do-dont-card src="/images/visual-simplicity-con.webp" alt="Hand holding a wallet with a a bunch of credit cards" color="danger">
Don't select cluttered scenes or images with too many competing focal points.
</image-do-dont-card>
</layout-container>

**Tip:** Simple images reduce visual noise and make the visual statement immediate and clear.
::

::step
#headline
Direct authenticity

#default
Use "pure" photography that has not been digitally manipulated or constructed.

<layout-container>
<image-do-dont-card src="/images/image-authenticity-pro.webp" alt="Authentic group of young people talking to each other" color="success">
Use images with naturally placed content.
</image-do-dont-card>

<image-do-dont-card src="/images/image-authenticity-con.webp" alt="Cross of four arms with an artificial 'Teamwork' text placed over the image" color="danger">
Don't use images on which text, graphics, or 3D elements were placed afterwards or that were created completely in 3D.
</image-do-dont-card>
</layout-container>

**Tip:** Every asset must feel captured by a camera, never constructed on a computer.
::

::step
#headline
Technical quality

#default
Verify that every asset meets high-fidelity technical standards before implementation. Additionally, the image resolution should be optimized for its usage since adding high resolution 4k image with a large file size that is displayed very small, leads to unnecessary high loading times and requires higher data volume (especially for cellular connections).

Also prefer modern file formats such as [WebP](https://developers.google.com/speed/webp/docs/cwebp) for reducing the file size.

<layout-container>
<image-do-dont-card src="/images/image-quality-pro.webp" alt="High resolution container ship" color="success">
Use high-resolution files that remain sharp on all screen types.
</image-do-dont-card>

<image-do-dont-card src="/images/image-quality-con.webp" alt="Very low resolution container ship" color="danger">
Don't accept assets with visible pixelation, compression artifacts or motion blur.
</image-do-dont-card>
</layout-container>

**Tip:** If individual pixels are visible, the image is not fit for use.
::

</steps>

## Accessibility

Images must have a descriptive alternative text to be accessible for all users. This ensures that users with visual impairments or those using screen readers can still understand the content conveyed by the image. It _can_ only be provided technically and must not necessarily be displayed visually. The alternative text is also used when the image can not be loaded.

## Technical usage

We recommend using our [OnyxImage](https://storybook.onyx.schwarz/?path=/docs/basic-image--docs) component for displaying images. It supports/requires accessible properties out-of-the-box and includes additional convenient features.
